# Vision

## The future we are building

Helicase is a new kind of scientific interface: a place where people can explore, question, and eventually create molecular systems through a continuous spatial experience.

Atlas is the first public expression of that vision. It turns a curated protein universe into a navigable field of stars, connects each star to a real structure, and gives the user a reasoning copilot whose output changes what is visible.

## The promise

Helicase does not hide scientific uncertainty behind polished language. It makes evidence, confidence, provenance, and imagination visible in the same interaction.

## Who it serves

- Curious people who need an intuitive entry into molecular biology.
- Scientists who want a faster way to inspect structural hypotheses.
- Engineers and designers exploring new interfaces for reasoning systems.
- Future research workflows that connect computational hypotheses to validated experiments.

## What success feels like

The user begins with wonder, asks a precise question, watches the scene respond, and leaves with a better mental model—not merely a transcript.

## What Atlas is not

Atlas is not a generic chatbot, a dashboard of disconnected metrics, a claim of therapeutic efficacy, or a replacement for experimental validation. It is a scientific instrument for exploration and hypothesis formation.
