# Spatial System

## Spatial metaphor

Atlas is a continuous observatory. The protein universe is not a background illustration; it is the primary navigable coordinate system.

## Scales

1. **Universe:** broad field, family clusters, implied scale.
2. **Neighbourhood:** related proteins and local context.
3. **Structure:** full molecular form and confidence.
4. **Domain:** selected functional region.
5. **Residue:** focused evidence and interaction site.

## Camera rules

Camera travel preserves direction and destination. The user should understand where they came from and why the next viewpoint matters. Avoid arbitrary spins and uncontrolled parallax.

## Anchoring

The identity rail and current-scale cue remain stable while the scene moves. Tool feedback should attach to the object or transition that caused it.

## Depth

Depth comes from scale, occlusion, light falloff, and focus—not from layers of floating cards. UI overlays should be sparse and spatially anchored.

## Performance principle

Spatial richness must be achieved through instancing, controlled effects, and predictable scene budgets. Visual ambition cannot make the scientific object unreadable.
