# Typography

## Typographic voice

Typography should feel editorial, engineered, and quiet. Large display type introduces a world; compact mono type identifies evidence.

## Roles

- **Display:** sparse, high-contrast statements for cold open and scene transitions.
- **Interface:** neutral sans for labels, controls, and explanations.
- **Evidence:** tabular or monospaced face for accession IDs, sequences, scores, residues, and pipeline stages.
- **Meta:** small uppercase or compact mono for source, confidence, and status qualifiers.

## Rules

- Never use display type for dense scientific data.
- Use tabular numerals for score comparison.
- Keep labels short; move explanation into the copilot or on-demand evidence layer.
- Maintain readable contrast in both bright structure scenes and dark map scenes.
- Do not use typography as decoration when a scientific label is needed.

## Scale

Use a restrained scale with one dramatic display size, two reading sizes, and a compact evidence scale. The exact font choices can be evaluated during implementation; the role system is the invariant.
