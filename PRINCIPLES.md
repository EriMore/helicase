# Principles

1. **Build an instrument, not a wrapper.** The scene, data, and reasoning must form one coherent system.
2. **Preserve wonder through rigor.** Cinematic presentation must increase understanding, not conceal uncertainty.
3. **Make causality visible.** Every meaningful GPT action should produce an observable scene change.
4. **Prefer a small number of excellent interactions.** Coherence beats feature count.
5. **Ground claims in structured evidence.** The copilot must distinguish retrieved facts, model predictions, and hypotheses.
6. **Treat uncertainty as information.** Confidence is a first-class visual and conversational state.
7. **Use real data where the product makes scientific claims.** Fixtures may curate; they may not fabricate provenance.
8. **Keep the architecture replaceable at the edges.** Providers and renderers can evolve; domain contracts should remain stable.
9. **Design for the first minute and the fifth year.** The demo must land immediately without making the codebase disposable.
10. **Challenge the brief when the evidence demands it.** A founder’s proposal is input, not immunity from critique.
